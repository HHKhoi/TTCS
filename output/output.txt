Many years ago, a drama teacher, exasperated at my bad acting in. a college play, shouted, "No! No! Your body is belying your words. Every tiny movement, every body position," he howled, "divulges your private thoughts. Your face can make seven thousand differ- ent expressions, and each exposes precisely who you are and what you are thinking at any particular moment." Then he said some- thing I'll never forget: "And your body! The way you move is your autobiography in motion.
